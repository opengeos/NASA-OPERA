# Welcome to the NASA OPERA Blog

Welcome to the blog for the [NASA OPERA](https://www.jpl.nasa.gov/go/opera) project website. This space is where we will share tutorials, release notes, and end-to-end workflow examples for working with OPERA data products.

## What You Can Expect

- **Tutorials** on accessing, processing, and visualizing OPERA products such as the Dynamic Surface Water Extent (DSWx), Disturbance (DIST), and Coregistered Single-Look Complex (CSLC) datasets.
- **Release notes** covering new examples, Jupyter notebooks, and changes to the companion code in this repository.
- **Workflow examples** that demonstrate how to combine OPERA products with open-source geospatial tools for real research questions.

## Join the Conversation

Each blog post includes a comments section powered by [Giscus](https://giscus.app), which is backed by GitHub Discussions. If you have a question, a suggestion, or want to share how you are using OPERA data in your own work, leave a comment on the post -- we would love to hear from you.

## Stay Updated

- Subscribe to the [RSS feed](/rss.xml) (or [Atom feed](/atom.xml)) to get new posts in your feed reader.
- Star or watch the [GitHub repository](https://github.com/opengeos/NASA-OPERA) to follow along as new examples are added.

Thanks for stopping by. More posts are on the way.
